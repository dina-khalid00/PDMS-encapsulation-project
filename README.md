# PDMS Encapsulation for Implantable Electronic Devices

## Overview
This project investigates the use of polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS) as an encapsulation material for protecting electronic circuits in saline environments, simulating conditions found in the human body.

Implantable electronic devices are exposed to moisture and electrolytes, which can lead to corrosion, electrical failure, and loss of functionality. This project focuses on evaluating simple and low-cost encapsulation methods to improve device durability.

## Objective
- Evaluate PDMS as a protective encapsulation material
- Compare different coating techniques
- Analyze performance of encapsulated vs non-encapsulated circuits
- Simulate real-world biomedical conditions using saline testing

## Background
PDMS is widely used in biomedical applications due to its:
- Biocompatibility
- Flexibility
- Chemical stability

## Materials
- LED circuits
- Resistance pads
- PDMS silicone (15:1 ratio base to curing agent)
- Beakers and petri dishes
- Oven (70°C curing)
- Saline solution (4% NaCl)
- Multimeter

## Methodology

### Circuit Preparation
- Built LED and resistor-based circuits
- Tested circuits before encapsulation
- Included uncoated control samples for comparison

### Encapsulation Methods
1. **Dip Coating (Single Layer)**
2. **Dip Coating (Double Layer)**
3. **Mold Casting (Full Encapsulation)**

### Testing Procedure
- Short-term immersion: 15 minutes
- Long-term immersion: 24 hours
- Observations:
  - Solution discoloration
  - Corrosion signs
  - Electrical performance

## Results

### Short-Term (15 minutes)
- No visible changes in any samples

### Long-Term (24 hours)
- **Uncoated circuits:**
  - Corrosion observed
  - Solution discoloration
- **Single dip coating:**
  - Minor degradation
- **Double dip & mold casting:**
  - No corrosion
  - No discoloration
  - Maintained functionality

Encapsulation significantly improved circuit protection. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## Discussion
- Double-layer coating and mold encapsulation provided the best protection
- Single-layer coating may contain microscopic gaps
- Unprotected circuits degrade rapidly in saline environments

## Conclusion
PDMS encapsulation is an effective method for protecting electronic circuits in harsh environments. Double dip-coating and mold casting are recommended for applications requiring long-term reliability in biomedical devices.
